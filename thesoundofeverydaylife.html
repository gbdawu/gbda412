mdi-cellphone Stream 2: Record Sounds With Your Cellphone or Field Recorder
Objective: Capture present-day sounds on campus to provide a current perspective.
  1. Go to a location near Stratford School to record an audio based on the theme you selected in Step 1.
    • If you choose to record with your cellphone:
      • Use your phoneâ€™s recorder to capture at least two to three different sounds related to your theme. Keep each recording short (30â€“60 seconds).
      • Rename your files descriptively (e.g., library_path_footsteps).
      • Tips for good recordings:
        • Hold your phone steady and close to the sound source.
        • Avoid covering the microphone.
        • Minimize background noise (wind, traffic, etc.).
        • Record multiple takes if needed.
      • Back in the classroom, transfer your recordings to your computer. (Options: email them to yourself, use AirDrop, Google Drive, or OneDrive)
      • If your recordings are not already in .mp3 or .wav format, convert them using CloudConvert

    • If you choose to record with the Zoom H6 field recorder:
      • Watch this short setup video: How to set up the H6.
      • Check that the SD card is inserted and that you have fresh batteries.
      • Choose the microphone capsule (X/Y or shotgun) based on your sound source.
      • Press Record once to arm the track and again to start recording (the red light will be steady).
      • Keep recordings short (30â€“60 seconds) and descriptive.
      • When finished, stop the recording and power off before removing the SD card.
      • Back in the classroom, transfer your files via the SD card reader or USB cable.
