GBDA 412 - Audio Cultures

Group Project 1: The Sound of Everyday Life (35% total)

Working in pairs (2 people), you will create a soundwalk for a site or path at Stratford School that reveals how people move through and experience that space. You should connect your soundwalk topic to topics and readings from weeks 1-4.

What is a soundwalk? A soundwalk is a form of active listening that focuses on the acoustic environment of a specific location or route. Participants walk through a space while paying particular attention to the sounds around them. Learn more

Overall Learning Outcomes

By completing this project, students will:

  1. Create site-specific sound art: Develop technical proficiency in field recording, audio editing, and location-based technology to produce original artwork that reveals new perspectives on familiar spaces.
  2. Collaborate effectively in creative contexts: Work productively with partners to synthesize individual concepts into cohesive artistic work, while providing and integrating constructive peer feedback throughout the creative process.
  3. Connect artistic practice to theoretical frameworks: Analyze how sound-based art can illuminate theoretical ideas about space, sound, and everyday experience.
  4. Reflect critically on creative learning: Articulate creative decisions, evaluate different artistic approaches, and analyze personal development throughout the project process using clear written and oral communication.
